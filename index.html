Mechanistic Analysis of Sparse Feature Representations in Transformer Residual Streams

Investigating the internal computational structure of transformer models by analysing sparse feature representations within the residual stream. I extract residual stream activations from pretrained transformers and train Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) to learn an overcomplete, sparse basis — decomposing dense representations into interpretable feature directions. Each learned feature corresponds to a direction in activation space whose magnitude signals the presence of a latent concept or pattern.

Mechanistic analysis involves identifying triggering contexts for specific features, tracing feature propagation across layers, and conducting causal interventions via selective ablation and modification of feature activations during forward passes. The goal: map how sparse features contribute to model behaviour, understand the computational circuits formed within the transformer architecture, and develop systematic tooling for feature-level interpretability.
